## Competitor Deep Dives

### 1. Legion Athletics — legionathletics.com

**Positioning:** "The most honest company in the supplement industry." Heavy on science — every formula cites peer-reviewed research. Long-form copy, ingredient-level education.

**Ad Strategy:**
- Heavy Google Search: targets high-intent ingredient keywords ("beta alanine supplement," "clinically dosed pre workout")
- Meta: primarily static image + carousel with ingredient callouts; long primary text (400+ chars) — unusual and works for their educated audience
- YouTube: long-form educational content (5–15 min videos) — content-led acquisition
- Strong email/SMS retargeting funnel

**Creative Themes:**
- "Clinically dosed. No underdosing. No proprietary blends."
- Ingredient education — explains WHY each ingredient is included
- Science citations in ad copy (e.g., "backed by 6 studies on pubmed")
- No lifestyle imagery — product-and-label focused

**Weaknesses for Proven. to exploit:**
- Zero gummy format — powder-only product range
- No taste messaging whatsoever — taste is assumed to be bad or irrelevant
- Creative is clinical and boring — low shareability
- No TikTok presence or native content
- Copy assumes high prior knowledge — inaccessible to casual gym-goer

**Keyword gaps:** "pre workout gummies," "best tasting pre workout," "easy supplement," "supplement on the go"

## Messaging White Space — Owned by Nobody

These angles are **completely uncontested** in the category. Proven. should plant its flag here:

1. **"The gummy that actually works"** — No competitor mentions gummies in performance context. This is Proven.'s category to create.

2. **"Supplements worth looking forward to"** — Every competitor ignores taste or treats it as an afterthought. Proven. can own the "supplements you actually enjoy taking" position.

3. **"Your gym bag upgrade"** — Convenience and portability are ignored by the entire category. Gummies in a gym bag beats scooping powder at 6am.

4. **"Clean for everyday athletes, not just elites"** — Momentous/Transparent Labs speak to elite athletes. Proven. can speak to the 95% who are "just" gymgoers, runners, weekend warriors.

5. **"The first supplement you'll actually finish"** — Powder tubs often sit half-used because they taste terrible. Gummies get finished. This is a genuine behavioral insight worth exploiting in UGC.

## Action Recommendations

1. **Own "performance gummies" before anyone else does.** This is a nascent keyword category with no brand established. Launch Google Ads targeting this cluster immediately and build brand association before a larger competitor moves in.

2. **Make taste the primary creative differentiator on TikTok.** Taste-reaction videos are natively shareable and require zero budget beyond product seeding to creators. This is the highest ROI move for a new brand.

3. **Target Legion and Transparent Labs audiences on Meta.** Interest targeting on Meta can layer "users interested in Legion Athletics" or "sports nutrition" + "clean eating" to reach the exact audience that already values what Proven. offers, but may be open to switching for format and taste.

4. **Launch TikTok before competitors notice the gap.** BPN is the only major competitor with any TikTok presence, and it's founder-led content, not performance advertising. Proven. can own the supplement gummy TikTok niche in 6–12 months if it moves now.

5. **Lead with convenience/portability on Google.** "Pre workout gummies," "supplement on the go," and "gummy supplements" are entirely uncontested. These should be the first non-brand ad groups live at launch.
